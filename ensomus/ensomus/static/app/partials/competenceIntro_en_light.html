Part 2: Skills Key

You have now come to the second part of the preparation guide. The Skills Key is a list of specially selected skills that the development dialogue contains.

Skills are about how you use your abilities, knowledge and proficiencies in the daily work tasks. There are two possible development paths; one is to get better at something you are already good at and the other is to develop completely new skills that are relevant to your work. Everybody possess the skills that are listed, but it differs to what extent we use and utilise them.

The purpose of the Skills Key is to gain knowledge and understanding of the skills that make the most sense for you to develop and focus on in relation to your work.

Preparation based on skills

The preparation is intended to get you to reflect on your skills in relation to your current and future work tasks, so you can get the focus it takes to develop them and solve your tasks in an even better way.

Note that you do not always have to increase your skills, but you may well be in a situation where you have to ease off your skills, it all depends on your experience, your personality and the nature of the task.
